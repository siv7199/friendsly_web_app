## Design Context

### Users
Friendsly serves fans who want more direct access to creators and creators who want a live-first, community-driven way to host, book, and grow recurring engagement. Fans browse creators, watch live sessions, queue for a turn, chat in real time, and book private calls. Creators need a product that feels active, trusted, premium, and easy to run while live.

### Brand Personality
Polished, human, and active. The product should feel warm, intentional, and contemporary rather than corporate, generic, or over-decorated. It should communicate momentum, presence, and community while still feeling premium.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a dark, editorial product language with warmer neutrals, refined contrast, restrained coral live accents, and sharper typography. Reference the uploaded images only for loose structure and product emphasis around discovery, live video, queueing, and chat. Avoid copying their layouts directly. Avoid generic AI dashboard styling, glassmorphism, repeated identical cards, purple-blue AI gradients, and overly soft blob-like UI.

### Design Principles
1. Make live presence visually central across discovery, creator profiles, and studio surfaces.
2. Use spacing and typography to create clear hierarchy before relying on decorative effects.
3. Let color communicate meaning: live, action, trust, and status should all feel deliberate.
4. Preserve every existing workflow and interaction while replacing the visual layer with something more cohesive and product-grade.
5. Make community visible: queue state, audience signals, chat, and return-visit cues should feel integrated, not secondary.
